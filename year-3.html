BBA Curriculum

Year Three: The Capstone of Financial Specialization

The final year of the BBA Finance program focuses on deep specialization and strategic integration, cultivating a "financial mindset" through advanced analytical rigor, strategic foresight, and ethical judgment.

Corporate Finance

This course is an advanced exploration of the fundamental financial decisions that drive a corporation's value, focusing on the complex trade-offs and analytical frameworks behind investment, financing, and dividend policies.

Curriculum Index:

  • Foundations & Investment Decisions: The goal of the firm, agency problems, corporate governance, and capital budgeting under uncertainty (risk analysis, decision trees).
  • Financing Decisions: Advanced estimation of component costs, Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC) and its application, and capital structure theories (NI, NOI, MM Propositions, Pecking Order).
  • Dividend Decisions: Issues in dividend policy, Walter's Model, Gordon's Model, Modigliani-Miller Dividend Irrelevance Theory, and determinants of dividend policy.
  • Long-Term Financing & Special Topics: Sources of long-term finance (equity, preference, debentures, term loans, lease financing), and Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A) including rationale, mechanics, valuation, and takeover defenses.
  • Working Capital Management: Advanced management of cash, receivables, and inventory (advanced models), and financing of working capital.

Investment Analysis and Portfolio Management

This central course provides a systematic framework for sound investment decisions, mirroring the professional investment process from macroeconomic analysis to individual security valuation and optimal portfolio construction.

Curriculum Index:

  • The Investment Environment: Introduction to investment, financial markets and instruments, and risk and return.
  • Economic and Industry Analysis: Macroeconomic analysis, industry life cycle analysis, and Porter's Five Forces Model.
  • Equity Valuation: Company analysis (financial statements, ratios) and equity valuation models (DDM, P/E Ratio, FCFE).
  • Debt Securities Analysis: Bond fundamentals (characteristics, types, yields) and bond valuation & risk (duration, convexity).
  • Portfolio Theory: Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT), Markowitz Portfolio Selection Model, Efficient Frontier, Optimal Portfolio Selection, Capital Market Theory (CAPM, SML, APT).
  • Portfolio Management & Derivatives: Portfolio performance evaluation (Sharpe, Treynor, Jensen's Alpha) and introduction to financial derivatives (forwards, futures, options, swaps, hedging).

Strategic Management

The ultimate capstone course, designed to integrate knowledge from all functional areas to understand how a firm achieves and sustains a competitive advantage from a holistic, C-suite perspective.

Curriculum Index:

  • Introduction to Strategic Management: Defining strategy, levels of strategy (corporate, business, functional), the strategic management process, and formulating vision, mission, and objectives.
  • Strategic Analysis: External environmental analysis (PESTEL, Porter's Five Forces) and internal environmental analysis (Resource-Based View, VRIO Framework, Value Chain, SWOT).
  • Strategy Formulation: Business-level strategy (Porter's Generic Strategies, Integrated Cost Leadership/Differentiation) and corporate-level strategy (growth, diversification, vertical integration, strategic alliances).
  • Strategy Implementation: Structural implementation (organizational structures) and behavioral implementation (leadership, corporate culture, control and reward systems).
  • Strategic Evaluation: Strategic evaluation and control, the Balanced Scorecard, key financial and non-financial metrics, and corporate governance and ethics.

International Financial Management

This course equips students with the knowledge to manage finance in a multinational context, addressing challenges and opportunities arising from different currencies, legal systems, and economic environments.

Curriculum Index:

  • The International Financial Environment: Introduction to the Multinational Corporation (MNC), the International Monetary System, and the Foreign Exchange (FOREX) Market (functions, structure, exchange rate determination).
  • Foreign Exchange Exposure: Managing transaction exposure (identifying, measuring, hedging with forwards, futures, options), and managing economic & translation exposure.
  • International Financing Decisions: International financial markets (Eurocurrency, International Bond Market, International Equity Markets).
  • International Investment Decisions: International capital budgeting (adjusting for political and economic risks, calculating NPV for foreign projects).
  • Short-Term Asset Management: International cash & working capital management (managing multinational cash flows, optimizing liquidity).

Income Tax – Law and Practice

This course provides essential, practical knowledge of the direct tax system in India, focusing on the Income Tax Act, crucial for financial planning, investment analysis, and corporate financial reporting.

Curriculum Index:

  • Basic Concepts: Key definitions (Assessee, Previous Year, Assessment Year), concept of Income, Residential Status and its impact on Tax Incidence.
  • Computation of Income: Income from Salaries (components, allowances, perquisites, deductions), Income from House Property (Gross Annual Value, deductions), Profits and Gains of Business or Profession (admissible/inadmissible expenses), Capital Gains & Income from Other Sources.
  • Aggregation and Deductions: Clubbing of Income & Set-off of Losses, and Deductions from Gross Total Income (Chapter VI-A: 80C, 80D, 80G, 80TTA).
  • Taxation and Procedures: Assessment of Individuals and Firms (computation of total income and tax liability), and Tax Collection and Filing (TDS, Advance Tax, ITR procedures).
